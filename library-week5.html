Family Worship

Lord’s Day 5 

Starting January 25, 2026 

* These elements are the best for families new to family worship. 

Call to Worship

Hear God call the family to worship and respond in unison:

Make a joyful noise to the Lord, all the earth!
Serve the Lord with gladness!
Come into his presence with singing!                            Psalm 100:1-2

Or:


Praise the Lord!
Oh give thanks to the Lord, for he is good,
His steadfast love endures forever!                                 Psalm 106:1

Confession

(Optional) Take a moment and give each family member an opportunity to confess their sins verbally.

The father prays on behalf of the family:

Almighty and most merciful Father,
   we have sinned against you
   and done what was evil in your sight.                          Psalm 51:4
We have done things we should not do,
   and we have not done things we should do.                
O Lord, have mercy upon us.                                            Psalm 51:1
Grant us to live a godly and righteous life,
   To the glory of your holy Name. Amen.

Assurance of Pardon

Receive the Word of God as the father reads:

God has said in His Word that:
We have an advocate with the Father,
   Jesus Christ the righteous.
He is the propitiation for our sins.                                    1 John 2:1-2
If we confess our sins, he is faithful and just 
   to forgive us our sins and to cleanse us 
   from all unrighteousness.                                                1 John 1:9

*Song of Adoration

Sing a song of your choosing with your family in worship to God.

Suggested song(s) of the week:
“"God Always Keeps His Promises" - Sovereign Grace Music

*The Requirements of God

The father holds out his fist and asks the question which the whole family responds while raising a finger for each answer:

What does the Lord your God require of you?
1. Fear the Lord your God
2. Walk in all his ways
3. Love him
4. Serve the Lord with all your heart 
   and all your soul
5. Keep the commandments and 
   statutes of the LORD                               Deuteronomy 10:12-13

*Gloria Patri or Doxology

Sing together:

Gloria Patri:
Glory be to the Father, and to the Son, and to the Holy Ghost;
As it was in the beginning, is now, and ever shall be,
World without end. Amen. Amen.

Or:

Doxology:
Praise God from whom all blessings flow;
Praise Him all creatures here below;
Praise Him above you heavenly hosts;
Praise Father, Son, and Holy Ghost. Amen.

Memory Verse

Either recite the memory verse together or have the children do it individually.

In the beginning, God created the heavens and the earth.         
Genesis 1:1

Bible Lesson or Catechism

The father leads the family in a Bible lesson or a catechism question.

 

Catechism Questions

12.Q.       Does God know all things?
A.              Yes. Nothing can be hidden from God.
                  1 Chronicles 28:9; 2 Chronicles 16:9; Luke 12:6, 7; Romans 2:16
13.Q.       Can God do all things?
A.              Yes. God can do all his holy will.
                  Psalm 147:5; Jeremiah 32:17; Daniel 4:34, 35; Ephesians 1:11

Bible Study

Weekly Bible study for older children/teens:

Colossians 2:1-5.

Weekly Bible study for younger children:

Day 1

Read Genesis 6:5-8.

Q. What did the Lord see about man’s sinfulness?
             A. That man was sinful in their heart continually.
Q. Did this make God angry and sad?
            A. Yes. It says that God regretted creating man on the earth.
Q. What did God want to do because of the sinfulness of man?
            A. He said He would kill all mankind and animals.
Q. However, there was one man that found favor in God’s eyes. What was his name?
            A. Noah.

Read Genesis 6:13-14,17.

Q. What is an ark?
            A. An ark is really big boat.
Q. Why was Noah going to need a really big boat?
            A. God said He was going to destroy the Earth with a flood.

Day 2

Read Genesis 6:18-22.

Q. Do you remember what a covenant is?
            A. A covenant is an agreement between two people in a relationship. For example, your mommy and I are in a covenant relationship we call marriage. We have agreed together to love one another no matter what until we die.
Q. Who was God making a covenant with?
            A. Noah and his family.
Q. What was God promising to do for Noah?
            A. He promised to bring him into the ark and keep Noah and his family safe during the flood.
Q. How many of each animal did God command Noah to bring on the ark?
            A. Two of each. Male and female.
Q. What did Noah do?
            A. He obeyed God and built the ark as God commanded him.

Read Genesis 7:1-6.

Q. How many clean animals did God command Noah to take into the ark?
            A. Seven.
Q. What is a clean animal?
            A. Clean animals were the animals God permitted His people to eat and to offer in sacrifices.
Q. How many days and nights did God say the flood rains would last?
            A. Forty days and forty nights.
Q. Do you know how long Noah and his family would end up being on the ark?
            A. Around an entire year.
Q. How old was Noah when he went into the ark?
            A. 600 years old.
Q. Do you remember why God is sending this flood?
            A. Because the sinfulness of mankind had grown so bad and this was God’s judgment on mankind.

Day 3

Read Genesis 8:1-5.

Q. What does it mean that God remembered Noah? Did God forget about Noah?
            A. It means that God remembered His covenant promise to Noah.
Q. How many days was the water on the earth?
            A. 150 days.
Q. Where did the ark first hit land?
            A. In the mountains of Ararat.
Q. Do you know how Noah discovered that the waters were completely dried up?
            A. Noah sent out a raven to fly. If the raven was able to find land where it could rest, the raven would not return. However, the raven went back and forth from the ark until the waters went away and it did not come back. Then Noah sent out a dove. The first time Noah sent the dove, it came back with nothing. The second time Noah sent out the dove, it came back with an olive branch showing that the waters were going away. The third time he sent out the dove, the dove found somewhere else to live and did not come back. That meant that the ground was dry and they could leave the ark.

Read Genesis 8:20-22.

Q. Noah made an offering to the Lord. Do you remember what an offering is?
            A. A gift of worship which we give to God.
Q. Was God pleased with Noah’s offering?
            A. He was pleased.
Q. God made another covenant. What did God promise?
            A. He will never again curse the ground or strike down all living creatures like He did here.

Read Genesis 9:12-17.

Q. What was the sign of God’s covenant with us?
            A. The rainbow.
Q. When we see a rainbow in the sky, what should we remember?
            A. That God has promised to never flood the whole earth in a flood again and we remember that God is gracious to us sinners.

*Prayer

Take turns letting everyone pray and conclude with the Lord’s Prayer in unison:

Our Father in heaven,
   Hallowed be your name.
Your kingdom come,
   your will be done,
   on earth as it is in heaven.
Give us this day our daily bread,
   and forgive us our debts,
   as we forgive our debtors.
And lead us not into temptation
   but deliver us from evil.
For yours is the kingdom, and the power,
   and the glory, forever. Amen.

*Apostles’ Creed

Confess together what you believe about the Christian faith:

I believe in God, the Father almighty,
   creator of heaven and earth.
I believe in Jesus Christ, his only Son, our Lord.
   He was conceived by the Holy Spirit
   and born of the Virgin Mary.
   He suffered under Pontius Pilate, 
   was crucified, died and was buried.
   He descended to the dead.
   On the third day he rose again.
   He ascended into heaven,
   and is seated at the right hand of the Father.
   He will come again to judge the living and the dead.
I believe in the Holy Spirit,
   the holy catholic Church,
   the communion of saints,
   the forgiveness of sins,
   the resurrection of the body,
   and the life everlasting. Amen.
